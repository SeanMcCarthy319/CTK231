SquareWaves Brand System

A full rebrand for a tech focused client that needed a visual system built on rhythm and clarity.

Role and Timeline

Role: Lead designer and art director.
Focus: Identity, visual system, UI direction, and launch assets.

Overview

SquareWaves came in with a name and a rough idea of their vibe, but they needed a cohesive brand system that could live across web, pitch decks, social, and product screens. The direction focused on modular blocks, wave inspired motion, and a calm but confident color palette.

Outcome

The final system gave SquareWaves a consistent voice across decks, product, and marketing. Clear hierarchy, flexible grid structures, and motion driven details helped the brand feel both technical and human.

Web Platform UX and UI

Responsive product design focused on clear flows, accessibility, and reusable design system components.

Overview

This project centered on simplifying a feature heavy web platform into a flow that felt intuitive for new users while still powerful for returning users. Attention was placed on layout rhythm, contrast, and a system of components that could scale.

Process

Wireframes and low fidelity flows were used to validate the structure, followed by UI design with a consistent type scale and spacing system. Components were documented and prepared for handoff to development with annotations.

Outcome

The updated platform achieved a cleaner information layout, better accessibility alignment, and a consistent UI library that could support future features.

Multisensory Branding for Accessibility

A research driven project that explores touch, sound, and scent as brand components that support inclusion.

Project Overview

This project investigates how brand identity can extend beyond visuals and into tactile, sonic, and scent based experiences. The goal is to design systems that are accessible and memorable for a wider range of users, including those who do not primarily rely on sight.

The work combines academic research, prototyping, and user feedback. It focuses on material selection, physical interaction, sound cues for digital products, and subtle scent environments that support brand recognition.

Process and Methods

The process includes research into multisensory branding examples, building tactile business cards, sonic identity sketches, and scent studies that align with a single brand story. Informal testing sessions were used to gather first impressions and emotional responses.

Learnings and Next Steps

Early findings show that multisensory cues can reinforce brand recall and emotional connection, especially when they are applied with intention and restraint. Future phases include more structured testing, refined guidelines, and additional examples for both print and screen.

Logo Collection

A set of marks designed for clients across tech, music, and local business.

Approach

Each logomark starts with a conversation about what the brand stands for and how it should feel. Sketches and iterations are used to reduce ideas down to simple, flexible forms that work in print, digital, and small scale applications.

Outcome

The collection shows range in industry and style while still feeling consistent in craft. Each mark is built to live inside a larger visual system and not just on its own.
